"Making sense of your financial future"

Goal

Program participants will gain an understanding of four basic financial literacy principles.


  • Banking

  • Budgeting

  • College Financing

  • Insurance

that complements the learning English finance terms lesson plan.





Vision

Program participants will gain a working knowledge of financial literacy components that will impact their everyday lives in a positive way as they learn introductory English. At the end of the learning program, participants will be confident in making Financial decisions that effect their everyday lives.





Mission

Implement a lesson plan on topics of finance that will establish a concrete financial literacy foundation for project participants of Maestro En Casa to be self-sufficiency and be confident making financially responsible decisions that are integral in their everyday lives.





Executive Summary

The Hispanic Austin Leadership (HAL) Finance Team is a collective group of working professionals with over 15 years of collective experience in the financial services field. The HAL Finance Team is focused on improving financial literacy in the Greater Austin, Texas Spanish speaking community. A financial literacy program was created by the team and is formally called “Making Financial Cents.” Through a partnership with Austin Independent School District (AISD), the pilot program will be implemented at four schools throughout the District that are currently teaching the “Maestro en Casa” Introductory English workshops to English Second Language (ESL) community stakeholders. AISD’s Maestro en Casa program is currently at 26 schools that has grown organically every year with over two-hundred graduates finishing the program in 2017. The English workshop has a financial vocabulary component which coincides with the “Making Financial Cents” curriculum. This seamless interaction between programs creates a unique and creative way to introduce financial education in a way that has never been taught in Central Texas. The topics in the financial program cover, Banking, Budgeting, Credit, Insurance and College Financing. The goal of the program is to grow alongside the “Maestro en Casa” Introductory English Program with an aggressive campaign of spreading across 24 AISD schools by 2020.

New Approach to Finance Learning

Our mission is to educate the community in basic financial topics that will establish a foundation for community members to be confident and take charge of financial decisions that affect your everyday lives. “Making Financial Cents” is a tool you can use to take the financial stress out of your mind and invest your energy in planning for your family’s future.

How much should I borrow?

You can determine whether you need a loan and how much you need to borrow by adding up the total cost of your education (tuition, fees, room and board, etc.) and subtracting the amount of scholarships, grants, and savings you have to contribute to those costs. You should borrow only what you need and consider the earnings potential in your chosen profession to determine how easily you'll be able to repay your debt. You can find salary estimates for a variety of occupations in the U.S. Department of Labor’s Occupational Outlook Handbook at bls.gov/ooh. Your student loan payments should be only a small percentage of your salary after you graduate.
